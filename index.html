A Breakdown of U.S. Inflation

By André Beganski

U.S. inflation worsened in February, rising to a 40-year high. The annual inflation rate rose to 7.9% last month, the Bureau of Labor Statistics said Thursday.

Its Consumer Price Index tracks price movements across a broad range of goods and services for consumers. Core CPI, an index that excludes food and energy because they are prone to volatile swings, shows how inflation is seeping into the broader economy at 6.8%.

The Federal Reserve, which controls U.S. monetary policy, is comitted to keeping yearly inflation at 2%.
